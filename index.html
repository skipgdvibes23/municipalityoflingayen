About Lingayen

In 1614, the Augustinian missionaries and Spanish conquistadores drew up a plan for Lingayen, marking its official founding. The town’s name, Lingayen, originated from a suggestion by the natives, inspired by a massive tamarind tree that once stood in the present-day town plaza. This tree was so exceptionally large and towering that it dwarfed the surrounding trees. Passersby were often captivated by its size, frequently looking back at it until it disappeared from sight. When asked which route they had taken on their way home, they would simply respond, through Liñgayen. This name was derived from the Pangasinan word "Lingawen," meaning to look back. Over time, the town officially adopted the name Lingayen, which it continues to bear today.

Lingayen later became the capital of Pangasinan when the province was designated as an encomienda during the Spanish colonial period.

During World War II, Lingayen played a significant role in history as the landing site of the Allied forces during the Invasion of Lingayen Gulf. Its expansive shoreline served as a crucial runway for attack planes, making it a strategic location in the liberation efforts of the Philippines.

Lingayen Beach, is a breathtaking public beach that offers free access to visitors. Unlike other popular beaches in the province, such as Hundred Islands in Alaminos, Patar Beach in Bolinao, and Tondol Beach in Anda, Lingayen Beach is known for its unique powdery grey sand. What makes it even more special is that cottages are available free of charge, allowing visitors to enjoy a full day by the shore without any fees.

Situated in front of the Provincial Capitol, Lingayen Beach boasts a long stretch of grey sand, well-maintained public bathrooms, and uniformly sized cottages. A spacious grass field before the shoreline provides a perfect space for children to play, making it one of the most organized public beaches in the province.

Beyond its scenic shoreline, Lingayen Beach also offers visitors a glimpse into history. Just a short distance away is the Veterans Memorial Park, a must-visit attraction showcasing photographs, war artifacts, and remnants from World War II. On display are actual fighter planes, tanks, and cannons used in battle, making it the most valued museum in Pangasinan. Visitors can also explore the Provincial Capitol of Pangasinan, which stands across from the park, further enriching their experience with the province’s rich heritage.
